## The Neurochemical Hijack  
### Lobotomizing the Mesolimbic System

The addiction mechanism is not metaphorical. It is **pharmacological**.

In a healthy brain, dopamine is regulated through **tonic firing**, maintaining motivation and baseline reward sensitivity. Social media platforms, however, induce **phasic firing**—sharp dopamine spikes that hijack the **D₁ receptor pathways** within the nucleus accumbens.

This pattern mirrors the neurological signatures of **gambling and narcotics**.

Each refresh becomes a conditioned anticipation loop. Over time, chronic overstimulation produces **structural degradation**:
- Atrophy in the **ventral striatum**
- Reduced volume and activity in the **prefrontal cortex (PFC)**

These regions govern impulse control, long-term planning, and decision-making.

Quantitative studies reveal that addicted adolescents exhibit **dopamine levels nearly double** those of healthy controls—**64.86 pg/ml** versus a baseline **36.79 pg/ml**—a biochemical marker of reward system dysregulation.
